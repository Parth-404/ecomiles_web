About EcoMiles

Why we built this

EcoMiles started as a first-year C programming project — a console tool to calculate CO₂ emissions from travel. It's now a full web application designed to help anyone understand and reduce the carbon cost of how they move through the world.

🌍 Our Mission

Transport is responsible for roughly 24% of global CO₂ emissions. Most people have no idea how much their daily travel choices contribute. EcoMiles makes this visible, understandable, and actionable — without guilt-tripping or preaching. We give you data. You make the decision.

📐 Methodology

How CO₂ is Calculated

Emissions are calculated using: Total CO₂ = emission_factor × distance

Per-person CO₂ is then: CO₂/person = Total CO₂ ÷ passengers

Emission factors are expressed in kg CO₂ per km per vehicle, not per passenger seat. This means carpooling (multiple passengers) reduces per-person emissions proportionally.

🌳 Tree Offset Calculation

Based on the widely-cited estimate that a mature tree absorbs approximately 21 kg of CO₂ per year. Trees needed = ⌈Total CO₂ ÷ 21⌉. This is a rough guide — absorption varies by species, age, and climate.

🎯 Eco Score System

A+ Under 0.5 kg CO₂/person — Exceptional. Walking, cycling.
A 0.5–2 kg — Excellent. Short train rides.
B 2–5 kg — Good. Bus, electric vehicle trips.
C 5–15 kg — Average. Solo car trips.
D 15–50 kg — Poor. Long car journeys alone.
F 50+ kg — Critical. Flights, heavy vehicles.
